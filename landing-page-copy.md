## Thesis

Agents are not replacing software. They are changing the shape of work.

The report examines the full AI stack: compute and data, models, agent infrastructure, applications, and society. It looks at where agents are producing repeatable value, where they remain brittle, and how they may reshape jobs, teams, wages, institutions, and who captures the gains.

## What the research covers

1. **Compute + data** — the capital, chips, datasets, and energy constraints shaping who can build at the frontier.
2. **Models** — frontier, open, local, and specialized models, and which capabilities matter outside benchmarks.
3. **Agent infrastructure** — tool use, memory, evals, orchestration, permissions, traces, and recovery systems.
4. **Applications** — workflows where agents are becoming products: coding, research, operations, support, sales, and personal work.
5. **Society** — the impact on jobs, management, skills, wages, institutions, regulation, and value capture.
