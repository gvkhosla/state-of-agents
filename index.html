State of Agents

A research report on how AI agents are moving from demonstrations to dependable systems.

Status
Forthcoming
Focus
Models, agents, infrastructure, applications, labor, society
Audience
Builders, operators, investors, researchers

Abstract

The report examines the full AI stack: frontier and open models, agent runtimes, tool and memory systems, application workflows, and the broader social consequences of delegation. It asks where agent systems are producing repeatable value, where they remain brittle, and how they may reshape jobs, teams, markets, and institutions.

The five-layer AI stack

01

Compute + data

The capital, chips, datasets, and energy constraints shaping who can build at the frontier.

02

Models

Frontier, open, local, and specialized models — and what their capabilities make newly possible.

03

Agent infrastructure

Tool use, memory, evals, orchestration, permissions, traces, and recovery systems.

04

Applications

The workflows where agents are becoming products: coding, research, operations, support, sales, and personal work.

05

Society

The impact on jobs, management, skills, wages, institutions, regulation, and who captures the gains.

Research questions

  1. Which model capabilities matter in practice, and which remain benchmark theater?
  2. Which agent workflows are reliable enough to use repeatedly?
  3. What separates production systems from impressive demos?
  4. How will agentic software change jobs, teams, wages, and managerial leverage?
  5. Where is value likely to accrue across the stack?
